Frequently Asked Questions

How does CarePlate work?
CarePlate connects restaurants with surplus food to NGOs that support people in need. Restaurants can register their leftover food on our platform, and NGOs can claim it for pickup or delivery, ensuring an efficient redistribution system.
How do I register my restaurant with CarePlate?
To register your restaurant, click on the "Restaurants" tab in the navigation menu and select "Register Restaurant." Complete the form with your restaurant details, upload necessary documents, and our team will review your application within 24-48 hours.
Are there any fees for using CarePlate?
CarePlate is free for both restaurants and NGOs. We operate on donations and grants to sustain our mission of reducing food waste and fighting hunger. However, we offer premium features for businesses that want enhanced services.
How do you ensure food safety?
We follow strict food safety guidelines. Restaurants must adhere to proper food handling and storage protocols. All food donations must meet safety standards, including proper temperature control during storage and transportation. We provide training resources to ensure all parties understand and follow these guidelines.
What types of food can be donated?
Restaurants can donate prepared meals, fresh produce, baked goods, and packaged foods that are still safe for consumption. We do not accept foods that have been served to customers, items past their expiration date, or foods that haven't been properly stored.
How is food transported from restaurants to NGOs?
NGOs can arrange pickup from restaurants, or restaurants can deliver the food directly. In some areas, we also have volunteer drivers who can help with transportation. All transportation methods must maintain proper food safety standards.
